Chapter 1

Foundations of Trigonometry

🔄 Angle Conversions
Degrees → Radians rad = deg × (π / 180)
Radians → Degrees deg = rad × (180 / π)
One full rotation 360° = 2π radians
📏 Arc Length, Sector Area & Speed
Arc Length s = r · θ    (θ in radians)
Sector Area A = ½ r² θ    (θ in radians)
Linear Speed v = r · ω    (ω = angular speed, rad/s)
Angular Speed ω = θ / t
📐 Right-Triangle Trigonometry — SOH CAH TOA
sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse
cos θ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
tan θ = Opposite / Adjacent
csc θ = Hypotenuse / Opposite    (reciprocal of sin)
sec θ = Hypotenuse / Adjacent    (reciprocal of cos)
cot θ = Adjacent / Opposite    (reciprocal of tan)

Chapter 2

Trigonometric Identities

🔁 Reciprocal Identities
csc θ = 1 / sin θ    →    sin θ · csc θ = 1
sec θ = 1 / cos θ    →    cos θ · sec θ = 1
cot θ = 1 / tan θ    →    tan θ · cot θ = 1

✏️ Worked Example

Given: sin θ = 3/5  —  Find csc θ
csc θ = 1 / sin θ = 1 / (3/5) = 5/3
Check: sin θ · csc θ = (3/5)(5/3) = 1 ✓
➗ Quotient Identities
tan θ = sin θ / cos θ
cot θ = cos θ / sin θ

✏️ Worked Example

Given: sin θ = 4/5,  cos θ = 3/5
tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = (4/5) / (3/5) = 4/3
cot θ = cos θ / sin θ = (3/5) / (4/5) = 3/4
Notice: tan θ · cot θ = (4/3)(3/4) = 1 ✓ (reciprocal pair)
🔺 Pythagorean Identities
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1    ← Primary identity
1 + tan²θ = sec²θ    → tan²θ = sec²θ − 1
1 + cot²θ = csc²θ    → cot²θ = csc²θ − 1

✏️ Worked Example

Given: sin θ = 5/13, Quadrant I  —  Find cos θ

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
(5/13)² + cos²θ = 1
25/169 + cos²θ = 1
cos²θ = 144/169
cos θ = 12/13

Verify derived identity:  1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
tan θ = 5/12,  sec θ = 13/12
1 + (5/12)² = 1 + 25/144 = 169/144
(13/12)² = 169/144  ✓
± Even / Odd Identities
Even (cos, sec) cos(−θ) = cos θ    sec(−θ) = sec θ
Odd (sin, csc) sin(−θ) = −sin θ    csc(−θ) = −csc θ
Odd (tan, cot) tan(−θ) = −tan θ    cot(−θ) = −cot θ
🔀 Co-function Identities  (complement = π/2 − θ)
sin / cos sin θ = cos(π/2 − θ)    cos θ = sin(π/2 − θ)
tan / cot tan θ = cot(π/2 − θ)    cot θ = tan(π/2 − θ)
sec / csc sec θ = csc(π/2 − θ)    csc θ = sec(π/2 − θ)
⚡ Quick Reference

Summary

Reciprocal

csc = 1/sin
sec = 1/cos
cot = 1/tan

Quotient

tan = sin/cos
cot = cos/sin

Pythagorean

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
1 + cot²θ = csc²θ

Even / Odd

Even: cos, sec
Odd: sin, tan, csc, cot

Co-function Rule

f(θ) = co-f(π/2 − θ)
for all 6 trig functions

Arc / Area

s = rθ    A = ½r²θ
v = rω    ω = θ/t
